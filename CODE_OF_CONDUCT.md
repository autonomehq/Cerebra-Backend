# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming community, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in Cerebra a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our project include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Showing empathy toward other community members
- Focusing on what is best for the community

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Violent language or threats of violence
- Personal insults, demeaning comments, or shaming
- Publishing others’ private information without consent
- Trolling, stalking, or sustained disruption of discussion

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this code of conduct. We reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this code of conduct.

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project team at **security@cerebra.dev**. All reports will be treated with discretion and respect.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community. Examples of representing the project include using an official project email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.
